Depreciation & Initial Direct Costs

Straight-Line Depreciation

Under an operating lease, the lessor retains the underlying asset on their balance sheet and depreciates it. The standard method is straight-line depreciation from cost to residual value over the asset's useful life.

Formula

Monthly Depreciation = (Asset Cost − Residual Value) ÷ Useful Life (in months)

Example

Asset Cost£100,000
Residual Value£20,000
Useful Life60 months (5 years)
Monthly Depreciation£1,333.33
Annual Depreciation£16,000

(£100,000 − £20,000) ÷ 60 = £1,333.33 per month

Useful Life Determination

The useful life is determined with reference to the following standards:

IFRS 16 S84

The lessor shall depreciate the underlying asset consistent with IAS 16 (Property, Plant and Equipment) or IAS 38 (Intangible Assets) as applicable.

ASC 842-30-35-5

The underlying asset shall be depreciated in accordance with the depreciation policy for similar assets held by the lessor (typically ASC 360).

Important: The useful life may be longer than the lease term. The depreciation period is based on the asset's economic useful life, not the lease term. This is a key distinction from IDC amortisation.

Initial Direct Costs (IDCs)

Initial Direct Costs are incremental costs directly attributable to negotiating and arranging a lease. Examples include:

  • Commissions paid to brokers or agents
  • Legal fees for lease negotiation
  • Costs of credit checks on the lessee
IDC Treatment for Operating Leases

Under both IFRS 16 S83 and ASC 842-30-25-11c:

  • IDCs are added to the carrying amount of the underlying asset
  • They are then expensed over the lease term on a straight-line basis (same basis as rental income)
  • This differs from depreciation, which is over the useful life of the asset

IDC vs Depreciation Comparison

Aspect Depreciation IDC Amortisation
Basis Cost less residual value Total IDC amount
Period Useful life of the asset Lease term
Method Straight-line (typically) Straight-line (same basis as income)
P&L impact Depreciation expense Amortisation of IDC expense

Tax Accounting Overview

Tax treatment of leased assets can differ significantly from the accounting treatment. Below is a high-level overview:

UK — Capital Allowances

  • Annual Investment Allowance (AIA): 100% first-year relief on qualifying plant & machinery (subject to annual limits)
  • Writing Down Allowance (WDA): 18% on the main pool or 6% on the special rate pool
  • Structures and Buildings Allowance (SBA): 3% straight-line for qualifying non-residential structures
  • Tax depreciation (capital allowances) replaces accounting depreciation for tax computation purposes

US — MACRS

  • Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System (MACRS): Prescribed recovery periods and methods (e.g., 5-year, 7-year property)
  • Section 179 Expensing: Allows immediate expensing of qualifying assets (subject to limits)
  • Bonus Depreciation: Additional first-year depreciation on qualifying property
  • Tax depreciation may differ from book depreciation, creating deferred tax differences
Confluence Sources
  • Operating lease accounting — straight-line depreciation to residual value, useful life determination (IFRS 16 §84 >> IAS 16 §50-62; ASC 842-30-35-5 >> ASC 360-10-35-2), IDC treatment (IFRS 16 §83; ASC 842-30-25-11), UK capital allowances, US MACRS depreciation
  • Operating lease accounting within agreement module — STRAIGHT_LINE_DEPRECIATION and STRAIGHT_LINE_CHARGE configuration for depreciation and IDC amortisation

Key Takeaways

  • Depreciation is calculated straight-line from cost to residual value over the asset's useful life
  • IDCs are expensed over the lease term, not the useful life
  • The useful life of the asset may extend beyond the lease term
  • Tax treatment (capital allowances / MACRS) often differs from book accounting
