Income Recognition

Straight-Line Recognition

The main idea is simple: even if the tenant pays different amounts each month, the lessor records the same amount of income every period. This is called straight-line recognition — you take the total rent over the whole lease and divide it evenly.

IFRS 16 S81

"A lessor shall recognise lease payments from operating leases as income on either a straight-line basis or another systematic basis. The lessor shall apply another systematic basis if that basis is more representative of the pattern in which benefit from the use of the underlying asset is diminished."

ASC 842-30-25-11

"A lessor shall recognise lease payments provided for in the lease, including any step rent provisions, on a straight-line basis over the lease term unless another systematic and rational basis is more representative of the time pattern in which use benefit from the leased property is diminished."

Example: Straight-Line Rental Income

Here's a 36-month lease where the rent goes up each year, but the income recorded stays the same every month:

Period Monthly Rental (Cash) Straight-Line Income Prepayment / (Accrual)
Months 1–12 £1,000 £1,167 (£167)
Months 13–24 £1,200 £1,167 £33
Months 25–36 £1,300 £1,167 £133
Total £42,000 £42,000 £0

Total rental: (12 × £1,000) + (12 × £1,200) + (12 × £1,300) = £42,000. Straight-line = £42,000 ÷ 36 = £1,167/month.

30/360 Day Count Convention

To keep things simple, RIAD pretends every month has exactly 30 days and every year has 360 days. This is called the 30/360 convention. It makes the maths cleaner — instead of worrying about February having 28 days, every month is the same:

Daily Rate Formula

Daily Rate = Total Amount ÷ Total Days (using 30/360)

Period Amount = Daily Rate × Days in Period

Example: Daily Rate Allocation

  • Total rental income: £12,000
  • Lease term: 12 months = 360 days (30/360)
  • Daily rate: £12,000 ÷ 360 = £33.33/day
  • Each month (30 days): £33.33 × 30 = £1,000
  • Final month: remaining balance to ensure total = £12,000 exactly

Revenue Processing Scenarios

Throughout a lease's life, different things happen — it starts, regular periods pass, and it eventually ends. RIAD has a specific record type for each stage:

Scenario Description
OPENING Zero-day opening record showing the initial state (no revenue recognised yet)
OPENING_WITH_FIRST_DAY_REALISED Opening record with Day 0 revenue recognised (early income recognition option for some jurisdictions)
PERIOD Regular period processing — applies the daily rate for the number of days in the period
FINAL Last period — recognises all remaining unearned rental income, unrealised depreciation, and unearned charges to ensure totals match exactly
FINAL_WITH_LAST_DAY_DEFERRED Last period with 1-day reduction (complements early income recognition on OPENING)
CLOSE_PERIOD Bridging period from last revenue date to a modification date — generated during lease modifications
Final Period True-Up: Because of rounding, small amounts can be left over. In the very last period, the system sweeps up whatever remains so the grand total comes out exactly right — no pennies lost or gained.

Variable Lease Payments

Some leases have payments that change based on things like inflation, interest rates, or how much the asset is used. The accounting treatment depends on whether the payments are truly unpredictable or just structured to look variable:

In-Substance Fixed Payments

Some payments look variable on paper but the lessee has no realistic way to avoid them. These are treated just like fixed rent — spread evenly over the lease.

True Variable Payments

Payments tied to actual usage or performance (e.g., a percentage of the lessee's sales) are not included upfront. They are only recorded as income when the event actually happens.

Lease Modifications

Sometimes a lease is changed mid-way — maybe the term is extended or the rent changes. How the lessor accounts for this depends on whether it counts as a brand new deal or just a tweak to the existing one:

ASC 842-10-25-15 — Modification Accounting

If the modification is not a separate contract, the lessor shall:

  1. Reclassify the lease (if needed) as of the modification effective date
  2. Account for any prepaid or accrued lease rentals as part of the new lease
  3. Treat the modified lease as a new lease from the modification date

RIAD Modification Processing

When a lease is modified under the RIAD method, the system:

  1. Creates a CLOSE_PERIOD from the last revenue date to the modification effective date
  2. Uses pre-modification daily rates to recognise revenue up to the modification date
  3. Recalculates forward-looking daily rates with the modified amounts and remaining term
  4. Generates new PERIOD records using the updated daily rates
  5. Maintains unearned balance continuity across the modification boundary

A modification is treated as a separate contract (i.e., a completely new deal) if both of these are true:

  • The lessee gets something extra that wasn't in the original lease (e.g., an additional asset), and
  • They pay a fair price for it — roughly what it would cost on its own

What If the Tenant Can't Pay?

If there's doubt about whether the lessee will actually pay, the rules change:

Scenario Income Recognition
Collectibility is probable Recognise rental income on a straight-line basis as normal
Collectibility is not probable Limit income recognition to the lesser of straight-line income or cash collected (ASC 842)
Collectibility subsequently becomes probable Catch up previously limited income to reflect the cumulative straight-line amount
Note: IFRS 16 doesn't spell out collectibility rules the same way ASC 842 does. Instead, any expected losses on lease receivables are handled under a separate standard (IFRS 9 — Financial Instruments).

Key Takeaways

  • Rental income is recognised on a straight-line basis over the lease term
  • Even if the rent goes up each year, the income you record each month stays the same
  • Variable payments based on usage are recognised when the event occurs, not straight-lined
  • Lease modifications (if not a separate contract) are treated as a new lease from the modification date
  • When collectibility is not probable, income recognition is limited to cash collected
  • RIAD uses a 30/360 day count convention with daily rate allocation
  • The final period performs a true-up to eliminate any rounding residue
