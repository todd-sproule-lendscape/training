Depreciation & Initial Direct Costs

Straight-Line Depreciation

Since the lessor still owns the asset, they need to account for it losing value over time. The simplest way is straight-line depreciation — you take how much value the asset will lose in total, and spread that cost evenly over its useful life.

Formula

Monthly Depreciation = (Asset Cost − Residual Value) ÷ Useful Life (in months)

Example

Asset Cost£100,000
Residual Value£20,000
Useful Life60 months (5 years)
Monthly Depreciation£1,333.33
Annual Depreciation£16,000

(£100,000 − £20,000) ÷ 60 = £1,333.33 per month

How Long Do You Depreciate For?

The “useful life” is how long you expect the asset to be economically usable. The accounting standards say:

IFRS 16 S84

The lessor shall depreciate the underlying asset consistent with IAS 16 (Property, Plant and Equipment) or IAS 38 (Intangible Assets) as applicable.

ASC 842-30-35-5

The underlying asset shall be depreciated in accordance with the depreciation policy for similar assets held by the lessor (typically ASC 360).

Watch out: The useful life is often longer than the lease itself. For example, a machine might last 10 years but the lease is only 5. You depreciate over 10 years (the full useful life), not 5. This is different from IDCs, which are spread over the lease term only.

RIAD Daily Rate Allocation

Rather than working in monthly chunks, RIAD breaks everything down to a daily rate. A component called TrackStraightLineAmount handles this for depreciation, rental income, and IDCs alike. Here's how it works:

How TrackStraightLineAmount Works
  1. Calculates a daily rate from the total amount and total days (using 30/360 convention)
  2. Applies days to each period — period amount = daily rate × days in period
  3. Tracks the remaining unrecognised balance (unrealised depreciation / unearned income) for future periods
  4. Handles rounding by carrying fractional amounts between periods
  5. Final period true-up — recognises the full remaining balance so the total matches the original amount exactly

Example: Daily Rate Depreciation

Asset Cost£100,000
Residual Value£10,000
Depreciable Amount£90,000
Lease Term36 months = 1,080 days (30/360)
Daily Rate£90,000 ÷ 1,080 = £83.33/day
Per Month (30 days)£83.33 × 30 = £2,500

The same daily rate approach is used for rental income and IDC amortisation, each with their own TrackStraightLineAmount tracker.

Initial Direct Costs (IDCs)

IDCs are the costs you only incur because you set up this lease. If the lease hadn't happened, you wouldn't have paid them. Common examples:

  • Commissions paid to brokers or agents
  • Legal fees for lease negotiation
  • Costs of credit checks on the lessee
IDC Treatment for Operating Leases

Under both IFRS 16 S83 and ASC 842-30-25-11c:

  • IDCs get added onto the asset's value on the balance sheet
  • They are then gradually expensed over the lease term (same even pace as rental income)
  • This is different from depreciation, which runs over the asset's full useful life — often longer

IDC vs Depreciation Comparison

Aspect Depreciation IDC Amortisation
Basis Cost less residual value Total IDC amount
Period Useful life of the asset Lease term
Method Straight-line (typically) Straight-line (same basis as income)
P&L impact Depreciation expense Amortisation of IDC expense

Tax Accounting Overview

The way you depreciate an asset for your financial statements is often not the same as how the tax authorities allow you to deduct it. Here's a quick summary of the key tax rules:

UK — Capital Allowances

  • Annual Investment Allowance (AIA): 100% first-year relief on qualifying plant & machinery (subject to annual limits)
  • Writing Down Allowance (WDA): 18% on the main pool or 6% on the special rate pool (note: main pool rate reduces to 14% from April 2026)
  • Structures and Buildings Allowance (SBA): 3% straight-line for qualifying non-residential structures
  • Tax depreciation (capital allowances) replaces accounting depreciation for tax computation purposes

US — MACRS

  • Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System (MACRS): Prescribed recovery periods and methods (e.g., 5-year, 7-year property)
  • Section 179 Expensing: Allows immediate expensing of qualifying assets (subject to limits)
  • Bonus Depreciation: 100% first-year depreciation on qualifying property (permanently restored by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, July 2025)
  • Tax depreciation may differ from book depreciation, creating deferred tax differences
Confluence Sources
  • Operating lease accounting — straight-line depreciation to residual value, useful life determination (IFRS 16 §84 >> IAS 16 §50-62; ASC 842-30-35-5 >> ASC 360-10-35-2), IDC treatment (IFRS 16 §83; ASC 842-30-25-11), UK capital allowances, US MACRS depreciation
  • Operating lease accounting within agreement module — STRAIGHT_LINE_DEPRECIATION and STRAIGHT_LINE_CHARGE configuration for depreciation and IDC amortisation
  • RIAD Revenue Processing Method — TrackStraightLineAmount daily rate allocation, rounding management, final period true-up

Key Takeaways

  • Depreciation is calculated straight-line from cost to residual value over the asset's useful life
  • IDCs are expensed over the lease term, not the useful life
  • The useful life of the asset may extend beyond the lease term
  • Tax treatment (capital allowances / MACRS) often differs from book accounting
  • RIAD uses TrackStraightLineAmount to manage daily rate allocation with rounding and final period true-up
